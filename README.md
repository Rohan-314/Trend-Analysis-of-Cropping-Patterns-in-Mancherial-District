# 📊 Trend Analysis of Cropping Patterns (2016–2019)

## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes agricultural cropping patterns in Mancherial District from 2016 to 2019 using data analytics and visualization techniques. The goal is to identify trends, seasonal variations, and land-use shifts to support data-driven agricultural planning.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze trends in crop area over time
* Identify dominant crops and pattern shifts
* Measure volatility and risk in crop selection
* Derive insights for policy and forecasting

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

* **Power BI** – Data visualization & dashboard creation
* **Microsoft Excel** – Data cleaning and preprocessing
* **Python (optional)** – Data analysis
* **SQL (optional)** – Data querying

---

## 📂 Dataset

* Government crop area data (Kharif & Rabi seasons)
* Data includes area sown, crop types, and yearly comparisons
* Dataset required cleaning due to inconsistent mandal naming

---

## 🔍 Methodology

1. **Data Cleaning**

   * Standardized mandal names
   * Reduced dataset inconsistencies

2. **Trend Analysis**

   * Analyzed crop area changes over 3 years

3. **Cropping Pattern Analysis**

   * Calculated percentage share of crops
   * Visualized shifts using stacked charts

4. **Correlation & Volatility Analysis**

   * Used Coefficient of Variation (CV)
   * Identified stable vs high-risk crops

5. **Visualization**

   * Built interactive dashboards in Power BI

---

## 📈 Key Insights

* **Cotton** dominated cropping patterns (~60% area)
* **Paddy** showed a declining trend over time
* **Red Gram** exhibited high volatility (CV ~75%)
* Strong competition observed between cotton and paddy for land

---

## 💡 Recommendations

* Improve policy support for stable crops like paddy
* Use pricing strategies (MSP) to stabilize pulse production
* Align agricultural planning with market trends (cotton demand)
* Encourage diversification with alternative crops



## 🚀 Future Scope

* Integrate climate and market data
